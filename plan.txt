My plan: The interaction study, but framed as an architecture paper

Nobody has published a systematic factorial study of how modern training-recipe components interact at small scale:
Muon × QK-norm, LayerScale × depth-wise residual schemes (AttnRes/mHC/value residual), z-loss × softcap × QK-gain.
Every one of these papers ablates its own component against a vanilla baseline; none ablate against each other's components.
A paper titled something like "Which modern transformer tricks are redundant?" with a clean 100M-param factorial design would
be novel, useful, citable, and impossible for me to be scooped on by accident — because the contribution is the experimental matrix,
not a single idea someone else can independently invent. This is my strongest option and I already have the platform built.
